## ACRE Africa, 2009-2015 (official, World Bank GIIF ACP report)

Premiums grew from 543 dollars in 2009 to 1,464,364 dollars in 2015. That
is a compound annual growth rate of 273.1% over six years. The number of
insured farmers grew from 185 to 394,426 over the same period, a CAGR of
258.8%.

Growth was not smooth. Premiums jumped 279% in 2011 and 134% in 2012,
then slowed to 17% growth in 2014 and 5% in 2015 as the program matured.
Margin as a share of premiums rose from 8.9% in 2010 to 23.9% in 2015,
showing the unit economics improved as scale increased.

Each premium dollar covered close to 10 dollars of farmer investment
through most of the period, rising above 21 dollars in 2013 during the
peak growth year.

## PlaNet Guarantee, West Africa (official, same report)

The Assurance Récolte Sahel project insured 52,228 farmers across Benin,
Burkina Faso, Cote d'Ivoire, Mali, and Senegal between 2011 and 2014,
beating its original target of 40,000. The team launched 14 new
insurance products and ran 12 workshops and training sessions. By the
end of the project, 5 insurers and 6 reinsurers carried PlaNet
Guarantee's products. In Burkina Faso, insured cotton farmers received
credit amounts more than 170% higher than farmers without insurance.

## CIMA and FANAF (official, cima-afrique.org and fanaf.org)

CIMA regulates insurance across 14 member states, most of them
francophone West and Central African countries, from its headquarters
in Libreville, Gabon. FANAF counts 203 member insurance companies and is
headquartered in Dakar, Senegal.

## Scale of the GIIF program (official, same report)

The GIIF ran active projects in 31 countries, 23 of them members of the
ACP group of states. The program's work reached 5 million people with
better access to index insurance products, and helped more than 1.3
million farmers and micro-entrepreneurs gain access to credit and
finance in sub-Saharan Africa. As of a 2011 estimate cited in the
report, microinsurance covered 44 million people across 39 African
countries, about 4% of the continent's population at the time.
